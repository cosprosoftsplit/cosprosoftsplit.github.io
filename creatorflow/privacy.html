CreatorFlow Privacy Policy

Effective 2026-05-18

Summary

CreatorFlow is a local-first content planning workspace. Your projects, ideas, brand kits, templates, and schedule dates are stored on your device using Apple SwiftData. We do not run a backend server, we do not have user accounts, and we do not collect, transmit, or share your project content with anyone.

What stays on your device

  • Idea inbox entries.
  • Content projects (drafts, ready-to-publish, published, archived).
  • Saved brand kits (voice notes, colors, CTA, banned words list, hashtag preset).
  • Reusable templates and generated drafts (captions, visual briefs, audio cue sheets).
  • Schedule dates (calendar-lite).
  • Optional brand reference images you attach from your photo library.

What we do NOT collect

  • No account creation, no email, no name, no phone number.
  • No analytics, crash reporting, or usage telemetry of any kind.
  • No location data, no contacts, no microphone or camera access.
  • No social-media account connections, no auto-posting, no scraping.
  • No third-party SDKs that could track you.

Photos library

If you choose to attach a brand reference image to a project, CreatorFlow asks for permission to read a single photo you pick. The selected image is copied into your device-local CreatorFlow database. We do not upload it anywhere.

Local notifications

If you opt in, CreatorFlow can schedule local reminders for project deadlines or schedule dates. These notifications are entirely local — Apple Push Notification Service is not used.

In-app purchases

CreatorFlow uses Apple StoreKit2 for an optional Premium lifetime unlock. Purchase, refund, and restore flows are handled entirely by Apple. We do not see your Apple ID, payment information, or any personally identifiable information.

Children

CreatorFlow is rated 4+. It does not knowingly collect any personal data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes

If we change this policy, the new version ships with the next app update.
